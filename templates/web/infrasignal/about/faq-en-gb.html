General

What is InfraSignal?
InfraSignal is a civic infrastructure reporting platform that connects United States residents with the local authorities responsible for maintaining public roads, sidewalks, streetlights, and other shared infrastructure. When you report a problem, InfraSignal automatically identifies the responsible city, county, or state agency and delivers your report to them for resolution.
Is InfraSignal free?
Yes. InfraSignal is free for all users. There is no charge to create an account, submit reports, receive status updates, or subscribe to local alerts.
Who operates InfraSignal?
InfraSignal is operated by PragmatTech. For any inquiries, visit our contact page or email [% c.cobrand.contact_email %].
What area does InfraSignal cover?
InfraSignal provides coverage across all 50 United States, the District of Columbia, over 3,000 county governments, and more than 25,000 city and town governments. When you place a pin on the map, the platform uses official geographic boundary data to determine which authority is responsible and routes your report accordingly.

Reporting a Problem

How do I report a problem?
  1. Enter a street address, city name, or ZIP code on the home page
  2. Click on the map at the location of the problem
  3. Select the category that best describes the issue
  4. Provide a description and optionally upload up to 3 photos
  5. Enter your name and email address
  6. Submit — InfraSignal routes your report to the responsible local authority
What categories of problems can I report?
InfraSignal supports 13 standard infrastructure categories:
  • Abandoned Vehicle
  • Bridge / Guardrail Damage
  • Drainage / Flooding
  • Fallen Tree / Vegetation
  • Graffiti / Vandalism
  • Illegal Dumping
  • Park / Public Space Issue
  • Pothole / Road Damage
  • Sidewalk Damage
  • Streetlight Outage
  • Traffic Signal / Sign Issue
  • Water / Sewer Issue
  • Other
What should I not report on InfraSignal?
InfraSignal is designed for non-emergency infrastructure issues. Do not use InfraSignal for:
  • Emergencies — call 911 immediately for any threat to life or safety
  • Crimes in progress or suspicious activity
  • Utility outages (gas, electric, water) — contact your utility provider directly
  • Noise complaints or neighbor disputes
  • Missed trash or recycling pickup
  • Building permits or code enforcement inquiries
  • Animal control
  • Parking or traffic enforcement
Do I need an account?
You must provide a valid email address when submitting a report so that InfraSignal can send a confirmation link and deliver status updates. You may also create a full account to manage all your reports and subscriptions from one dashboard.
Can I upload photos?
Yes. You may attach up to 3 photos per report. Photos are automatically screened for inappropriate content before being published.
Can I report anonymously?
You may choose to hide your name from the public-facing report page. However, your name and email address are always shared with the responsible local authority so they can investigate and, if needed, contact you about the issue.

After Submitting

How are reports delivered to local authorities?
Reports are sent via email to the designated contact address for the responsible local authority. Each report includes the problem location (coordinates and address), the selected category, your description, any attached photos, and your contact information.
How are reports prioritized?
InfraSignal assigns one of four priority levels to each report: Emergency (immediate safety hazard), High (needs attention within 24 hours), Normal (standard response time), and Low (non-urgent). Local authorities can adjust priority as part of their investigation.
How long will it take to fix my problem?
Response times depend on the category, severity, and the local authority’s workload. InfraSignal does not control how quickly authorities respond; it enables you to track your report’s status and receive email updates when action is taken.
How do I check the status of my report?
You will receive email notifications when the status changes. You can also log in to your account and view all your reports under Your account. Each report shows its current status: open, investigating, in progress, fixed, or closed.
What if my problem has not been addressed?
You can post an update on your existing report at any time to provide additional photos, note that the problem persists, or add new details. The responsible authority is notified of each update.
Can I see reports submitted by others?
Yes. The All Reports page displays all public reports with their status, location, and category. You can browse by area or search for specific locations.
Can I get alerts about problems near me?
Yes. Visit Local Alerts to subscribe to email notifications for new reports in your area. You can set alerts by geographic radius or by specific local authority.

For Local Authorities

How do we receive reports?
Reports are delivered to the contact email address registered for your jurisdiction. Each email contains the full report details including location coordinates, category, description, priority, photos, and reporter contact information. To update your contact details, please use our contact form.
Does InfraSignal support Open311?
The platform is built on technology that supports the Open311 GeoReport v2 standard. If your agency uses a CRM or work order system with Open311 compatibility, we can work with you to configure automated integration. Contact us to discuss setup.
Is there a cost for local authorities?
No. Receiving and responding to reports through InfraSignal is free for all government agencies.
How is jurisdiction determined?
InfraSignal uses official geographic boundary data to map each report’s location to the responsible city, county, or state authority. Reports are routed to the most specific jurisdiction available.

Technical

What devices are supported?
InfraSignal is a responsive web application that works on smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers. No app installation is required — access through any modern web browser.
What map does InfraSignal use?
InfraSignal uses OpenStreetMap for mapping and location services. OpenStreetMap is a free, community-maintained geographic database.
Is the platform open source?
The underlying platform technology is available under the GNU Affero General Public License (AGPL v3). The software source code can be inspected, modified, and deployed by anyone, in accordance with the license terms.
