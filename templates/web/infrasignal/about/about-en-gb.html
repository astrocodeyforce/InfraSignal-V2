About InfraSignal

InfraSignal is a civic infrastructure reporting platform that makes it simple for residents to report and track local problems — and for local authorities to receive, prioritize, and resolve them.

What We Do

Across the United States, infrastructure problems like potholes, broken streetlights, damaged sidewalks, and illegal dumping go unreported because residents don’t know who to contact or how to reach them. InfraSignal solves this by providing a single, easy-to-use platform that automatically routes reports to the correct city, county, or state authority based on the geographic location of the problem.

When a resident submits a report, InfraSignal:

  1. Identifies the responsible local authority using official geographic boundary data
  2. Delivers the complete report — including location, category, description, photos, and priority level — directly to that authority
  3. Keeps the reporter updated with email notifications as the status changes
  4. Publishes the report on a public map so the community has visibility into local issues and their resolution

Coverage

InfraSignal currently serves the entire United States, with infrastructure reports routed across:

  • 51 state-level authorities (all 50 states and the District of Columbia)
  • Over 3,000 county governments
  • More than 25,000 city and town governments

All jurisdictions receive reports across 13 standardized infrastructure categories, including pothole / road damage, streetlight outage, drainage / flooding, graffiti / vandalism, sidewalk damage, bridge / guardrail damage, traffic signal / sign issues, and more.

How It Works

Step What Happens
1. Locate Enter a street address, city, or ZIP code. Place a pin on the map at the problem location.
2. Describe Select a category, write a description, and upload up to 3 photos. Photos are automatically checked for inappropriate content.
3. Route InfraSignal determines the responsible authority using geographic boundary data and sends your report with full details and priority classification.
4. Track Receive email updates as the authority investigates. View all reports on the public map. Post follow-up updates at any time.

For Local Authorities

InfraSignal gives government agencies a free, standardized channel to receive infrastructure reports from constituents. Benefits include:

  • Structured, geolocated reports with photos and priority levels
  • 13 standardized categories aligned with common public works operations
  • Four-tier priority system (Emergency, High, Normal, Low) created automatically for each jurisdiction
  • Compatibility with the Open311 GeoReport v2 standard for future automated integration with CRM and work order systems
  • No cost to receive, manage, or respond to reports
